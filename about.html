Who We Are: The Unified Front

STOP OPERATING IN SILOS. START BUILDING POWER.

Gender specialists around the world work with policymakers, governments, media, non-profit organizations, and businesses. They handle a range of crucial activities: from gender mainstreaming and inclusion to program design, research, evaluation, workplace diversity, LGBTQI+ advocacy, and organizational auditing.

If you are applying an intersectional gender lens in your work or activism, The Society of Gender Professionals (SGP) is meant to be a community for you. We are the only cross-cutting association that currently exists to professionalize the gender field and meet the needs of a growing group of gender specialists across sectors and around the world.

We are a non-profit membership network of practitioners, academics, and activists dedicated to promoting feminist action and applied research, and raising the profile of gender expertise.

Our Six Revolutionary Strives:

  • 01.

    Promote the development of intersectional gender expertise for professional practice.

  • 02.

    Strengthen connections between gender theory, policy, and practice.

  • 03.

    Promote member collaboration, network-building, mentorship, and (co-)leadership.

  • 04.

    Foster the production and dissemination of applied gender research.

  • 05.

    Provide a platform for the exchange of gender expertise and resources.

  • 06.

    Advance gender justice in member communities through coordinated feminist action.

SGP’s vision is to be a global, cross-sectoral association of gender specialists, where members are empowered with the resources and networks they need to thrive in their careers and advance gender justice in their communities.

THE THREE PILLARS OF SGP POWER

THE ACADEMIC REVOLUTION

Researchers and academics who work on issues related to gender justice and equality often operate in silos. SGP welcomes academics from all fields—from political science and economics to anthropology, sociology, and women’s studies—to share their expertise, collaborate, and contribute to a nuanced understanding of gender norms worldwide.

WHY SGP EXISTS

FORMALIZING THE POST-BEIJING POWER SHIFT.

The need for gender expertise has grown exponentially since the Fourth World Conference on Women held in Beijing, China in 1995. That year, the world reaffirmed the urgent need to protect the human rights of women and governments committed to systematically address gender inequalities in their societies through gender mainstreaming and gender integration.

Since then, Gender Specialists have been recruited by governments, companies, and nonprofit organizations to help formulate policies, design programs, evaluate activities, train, teach, and mentor.

As the field of gender expertise grows, so does our need to formalize, organize, and standardize the profession. New Gender Specialists often lack resources, networks, mentors, training, frameworks, and access to research. SGP solves this critical void.

Honorarium Policy (Effective Sept 2022)

This policy provides financial support for those providing labor on SGP anti-racism and/or anti-oppression activities. Per the Racial Justice Task Force report, certain identities are prioritized for honoraria, including Black-identifying or racialized people (BIPOC/BAME) and others harmed by oppression based on identity and social location.

Professional Development Policy (Effective Nov 2022)

Outlines eligibility criteria for the distribution of professional development funds to members in need of financial support.
